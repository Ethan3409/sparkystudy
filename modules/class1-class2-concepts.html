Quick Overview

▼
CEC Section 16 — Class 1 & Class 2 Circuits

Section 16 covers circuits that can use conductors smaller than No. 14 AWG because their current and voltage are limited enough to not present serious shock or fire hazards. These are the low-power, low-voltage circuits you will find everywhere in modern buildings.

The 4 Circuit Types

▼
🔌

Remote Control Circuit

Class 1 or 2

Controls another circuit through a relay.

  • Float switch controlling a pump via relay coil/contact
  • Can be Class 1 OR Class 2
💡 "Remote = Relay" — both start with R
🔔

Signal Circuit

Class 1 or 2

Energizes signaling equipment (bells, buzzers, alarms).

  • Push button → bell/buzzer
  • Can be Class 1 OR Class 2
  • NOT the same as communication circuits!
💡 "Signal = Sound" — both start with S
⚡

Extra-Low Voltage Power Circuit

Class 1 Only

Power circuit limited to 30 V max, 1000 VA max.

  • Float switch controlling a water valve solenoid
  • CLASS 1 ONLY
💡 "Extra-Low = Extra restrictions = Class 1 only"
🔋

Low-Energy Power Circuit

Class 2 Only

Power circuit limited to 150 V max, 100 VA max.

  • Float switch controlling a water valve solenoid at lower power
  • CLASS 2 ONLY
💡 "Low-Energy = Laid back rules = Class 2"

💡 18 goes with 5, 16 goes with 10. The BIGGER wire gets the BIGGER protection.

Class 2 Overcurrent Protection

I = VA / V → I = 100 VA / V
At 24 V:   I = 100 / 24 = 4.16 A
At 54 V:   I = 100 / 54 = 1.85 A
💡 Class 2 always divides into 100 VA. Higher voltage = LOWER current allowed.

Conductor Types & Ampacities

▼

TEW — Equipment Wire

Used in raceways for Class 1 circuits
  • Available in 300 V or 600 V insulation
  • Used per Rule 16-112 and Table 11
  • Can be pulled into raceways

LVT — Low-Voltage Thermostat Cable

Has a non-metallic sheath
  • OK for heating control circuits
  • No. 18 AWG, 2-conductor = 5 A (Table 57)
  • No. 16 AWG, 4-conductor = 10 A × 0.8 = 8 A (derating for >3 conductors)

ELC — Extra-Low Voltage Cable (aka "bell wire")

NO sheath — twisted conductors
  • Must NOT be used for heating control or fire safety circuits
  • Think of it as the simplest/cheapest option
💡 "ELC = Exclude from Life-Critical" (heating/fire)
💡 Derating Rule: More than 3 conductors in a cable → multiply ampacity by 0.8. "More than 3? Multiply by point-8!"

Separation Rules

▼

Rule 16-212 — Keep Class 2 Separated

Class 2 wiring must be kept 50 mm from lighting, power, and Class 1 conductors.

Metal barriers may be used in shared enclosures as an alternative to the 50 mm spacing.

💡 50 mm = about 2 inches — keep Class 2 two inches from power
7

Grounding Rules (Rule 10-206)

▼

Over 30 V → MUST be solidly grounded

No exceptions. If it exceeds 30 V, ground it.

30 V or Less → Must be grounded IF:

  • 1. Run overhead outside
  • 2. Supplied by transformer with primary >150 V to ground
  • 3. Supplied by transformer with ungrounded primary
💡 "Over 30? Ground it for sure. Under 30? Only if it goes outside or has a big transformer."
