Circuit Explanation

How it Works:

Rung 1 (Control): STOP button (NC) in series with START button (NO) control the motor contactor coil (M). When START is pressed and STOP is released, the coil energizes.

Rung 2 (Seal-in): M contact parallel to START button. Once the coil energizes, the M contact closes and "seals-in," maintaining coil energization even after START is released.

Overload Protection: OL relay monitors motor current. If current exceeds the threshold (40A), the OL contact opens and de-energizes the coil, stopping the motor.

Motor: When contactor closes, power flows to the motor. The motor creates mechanical rotation proportional to applied load.

Fault Scenarios

Scenario 1: Normal Operation

Start the motor, monitor current at 10A. What happens when you press STOP?

Scenario 2: Overload Trip

Increase current beyond 40A. Observe OL relay tripping the circuit automatically.

Scenario 3: Stuck START Button

Press START. The seal-in contact maintains operation. Manually increase current to trip OL.
