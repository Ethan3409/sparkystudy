Rationale

Why is it important for you to learn this skill?

Alternating current (ac) circuits are widely used as the means of distribution for electric
energy. Most of your work involves ac circuits. You were introduced to ac resistive
circuits in first period training. In second period training, you will learn about additional
ac circuit properties that are crucial to your understanding of the electrical theory and the
electrician trade.

Outcome

When you have completed this module, you will be able to

Describe the fundamentals of alternating current (ac).

Objectives

1. Describe the generation of an ac sine wave.
2. Determine the output frequency of an ac generator.
e 3. Calculate standard ac sine wave values.

Introduction

This module discusses the generation of an alternating current and shows how a graph
called a sine wave is generated. It also describes the relationship between output
frequency, poles, and rotational speed. Finally, the module explains effective (RMS)
voltage and performs sine wave calculations. This module will prepare you for further
exploration of ac circuits in second period theory.
